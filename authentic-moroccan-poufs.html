Care and Maintenance of Moroccan Poufs

Proper care and maintenance of Authentic Moroccan Poufs ensures their longevity and beauty. For vibrant kilim poufs, regular vacuuming helps remove dust, maintaining the integrity of the intricate patterns. For small poufs and larger pieces like round leather poufs, occasional wiping with a damp cloth is recommended, as leather can absorb dirt. A perfect leather pouf should be conditioned periodically to keep the material supple and prevent cracking. For poufs stuffed with natural materials, avoiding excessive moisture is crucial to preserve their shape and comfort. Rectangular leather poufs require attention to corners, preventing wear from rubbing against surfaces. By following these guidelines, you can enjoy the charm of your Authentic Moroccan Poufs for many years.

  • Regularly vacuum kilim poufs to remove dust and debris.
  • Wipe leather poufs with a damp cloth to avoid dirt buildup.
  • Condition leather poufs every few months to keep them supple.
  • Avoid placing poufs in humid environments to prevent moisture damage.
  • Check and maintain the corners of rectangular poufs to minimize wear.
  • Rotate your poufs occasionally to ensure even wear and usage.
  • Store poufs in a cool, dry place when not in use to maintain their shape.
